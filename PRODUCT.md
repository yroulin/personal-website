# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences: enterprise hiring managers and AEM clients scanning candidates for competence, and developers in the Bitcoin / self-hosting / open source community looking for genuine alignment. Both need to feel this was made by a real craftsperson, not assembled from a template.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Yves Roulin, Front End Developer specializing in Adobe Experience Manager. Exists to communicate credibility to potential employers and authentic identity to technical peers. Success: a visitor leaves knowing exactly who Yves is and what he stands for.

## Brand Personality

Precise, Authentic, Opinionated. Not shy about Bitcoin, open source, or self-hosting convictions. Warmth comes through specificity (family, cooking with his son, yoga) not through decoration or forced cheerfulness.

## Anti-references

- The current site's own patterns: gradient text, glassmorphism nav, decorative ambient blobs
- Generic SaaS landing page aesthetic: cream background, floating orbs, identical card grids
- Crypto neon maximalism: neon on black, excessive gradients, "degen" energy
- Corporate resume page: white background, zero personality, looks like a LinkedIn print export
- Over-animated agency sites: motion for motion's sake, everything parallaxing

## Design Principles

1. Every element earns its place, or it leaves
2. Personality through content specificity, not surface decoration
3. Dark, readable, unhurried: typographic clarity over visual noise
4. Accessibility is non-negotiable, not an afterthought
5. Escape the reflex: if a developer portfolio could be described as "dark with purple/teal gradients," it has failed

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` at the system level plus expose a manual motion toggle in the UI. Color system validated for deuteranopia and protanopia. No information encoded in color alone. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus states.
